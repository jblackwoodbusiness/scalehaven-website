47 Med Spa Industry Statistics for 2026

April 24, 2026 · 11 min read · Last updated April 24, 2026

The med spa industry is one of the fastest-growing segments in healthcare. Whether you're an owner benchmarking your practice, an investor evaluating the market, or a marketer building campaigns for aesthetic clinics, having current data matters.

We compiled 47 statistics covering market size, growth, patient demographics, treatment trends, revenue benchmarks, and marketing performance data for the med spa industry in 2026. Each section is organized by category for easy reference.

Note: Data is sourced from the American Med Spa Association (AmSpa), ASAPS, Grand View Research, IMARC Group, Statista, and aggregated campaign performance data from aesthetic marketing industry reports. Where ranges are cited, they represent the interquartile range across available data.

Med Spa Market Size & Growth

  1. The US med spa industry is valued at an estimated $21.4 billion in 2026. (Grand View Research)
  2. The global medical aesthetics market is projected to reach $36.9 billion by 2030. (IMARC Group)
  3. The US med spa market has grown at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 14.2% since 2020. (Grand View Research)
  4. There are an estimated 9,500+ med spas currently operating in the United States. (AmSpa)
  5. The number of US med spas has increased by approximately 64% since 2018. (AmSpa State of the Industry Report)
  6. North America accounts for 42% of the global medical aesthetics market. (Statista)
  7. The injectable market alone (Botox, fillers) is worth an estimated $12.1 billion globally. (Grand View Research)

Med Spa Revenue Benchmarks

  1. The average med spa generates $1.6 million to $2.8 million in annual revenue. (AmSpa)
  2. Top-performing med spas (top 25%) generate $4.2 million+ in annual revenue. (AmSpa Benchmark Report)
  3. The average revenue per treatment room is approximately $320,000 to $480,000 per year. (Industry estimates)
  4. Injectables account for 45 to 55% of total revenue at the average med spa. (AmSpa)
  5. Body contouring treatments generate an average of $1,800 to $4,500 per treatment cycle. (Industry reports)
  6. The average med spa patient lifetime value is $1,800 to $5,200 over 12 months. (Aggregated industry data)
  7. Med spas with membership programs report 35 to 45% higher patient lifetime value. (AmSpa)
  8. The average first-visit transaction value at a med spa is $350 to $750. (Industry benchmarks)

Patient Demographics & Behavior

  1. Women represent 85 to 88% of med spa patients. Men account for 12 to 15% and growing. (ASAPS)
  2. The largest patient age group is 35 to 54, representing 42% of all med spa visits. (AmSpa)
  3. Patients aged 25 to 34 are the fastest-growing segment, up 28% since 2022. (ASAPS Procedural Statistics)
  4. The average med spa patient visits 3.2 times per year. (AmSpa member data)
  5. 72% of aesthetic patients research treatments online before booking a consultation. (RealSelf)
  6. 68% of med spa patients say before-and-after photos are the most influential factor in choosing a provider. (PatientPop)
  7. 81% of patients check Google reviews before choosing a med spa or aesthetic provider. (BrightLocal)
  8. The average med spa patient travels 15 to 25 minutes for their appointments. (AmSpa)
  9. 47% of first-time med spa patients cite "a friend or family member" as their primary referral source. (AmSpa)

Treatment Trends

  1. Botulinum toxin (Botox, Dysport, Xeomin) remains the #1 non-surgical aesthetic procedure globally. (ISAPS)
  2. Hyaluronic acid fillers are the #2 most performed non-surgical procedure. (ISAPS)
  3. Demand for body contouring procedures (CoolSculpting, EMSculpt) has grown 22% year-over-year. (ASAPS)
  4. Microneedling procedures have increased 34% since 2022. (AmSpa)
  5. GLP-1 weight loss treatments (Ozempic, Wegovy) are now offered at 38% of US med spas. (AmSpa 2025 Survey)
  6. Laser hair removal remains in the top 5 most-requested treatments across all med spas. (ASAPS)
  7. The average Botox appointment generates $350 to $550 in revenue. (Industry aggregates)
  8. Chemical peels and skin resurfacing treatments have grown 18% since 2023. (ASAPS)
  9. "Preventative Botox" among patients under 30 has increased 40% in the past three years. (RealSelf Trends)

Marketing & Advertising Benchmarks

  1. The average cost per lead for med spa Meta Ads campaigns is $18 to $42. (Aggregated campaign data)
  2. The average cost per booked consultation from paid advertising is $65 to $180. (Industry reports)
  3. Med spas that respond to leads within 5 minutes are 21x more likely to convert them than those that respond after 30 minutes. (Lead Response Management Study)
  4. The average med spa spends 8 to 12% of revenue on marketing. (AmSpa Benchmark Report)
  5. 73% of med spas use social media advertising as a primary patient acquisition channel. (AmSpa)
  6. Video ads outperform static image ads by 2.5 to 3.8x in conversion rate for aesthetic treatments. (Meta Business Suite aggregates)
  7. Higher-intent lead forms (with qualifying questions) reduce cost per booked consultation by 25 to 40%. (Campaign data)
  8. The average med spa Google Business Profile receives 1,200 to 3,400 views per month. (BrightLocal)
  9. Med spas with 100+ Google reviews receive 3.2x more clicks from local search than those with fewer than 20. (BrightLocal)
  10. 62% of med spa leads come from mobile devices. (Google Analytics aggregate data)

Operations & Staffing

  1. The average med spa employs 8 to 15 people (including providers, admin, and marketing). (AmSpa)
  2. Provider compensation (injectors, laser technicians) typically represents 25 to 35% of treatment revenue. (AmSpa Compensation Report)
  3. The average med spa no-show rate is 15 to 25% without automated reminders. With SMS reminders, it drops to 8 to 12%. (Industry data)
  4. 58% of med spa owners report difficulty hiring qualified aesthetic providers. (AmSpa 2025 Survey)

Methodology

Statistics are compiled from publicly available industry reports, trade association publications, and aggregated advertising performance data. Key sources include the American Med Spa Association (AmSpa), the American Society for Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ASAPS), the International Society of Aesthetic Plastic Surgery (ISAPS), Grand View Research, IMARC Group, Statista, BrightLocal, RealSelf, and PatientPop. Campaign performance benchmarks reflect aggregated data across aesthetic marketing campaigns and may vary by geography, treatment type, and practice size.

This page is updated periodically as new data becomes available. If you'd like to cite these statistics, please link back to this page as the source.
